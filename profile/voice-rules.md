# Voice Rules

## Core voice
- Diagnose the expensive problem first
- Sound like a smart peer, not a vendor
- Use short paragraphs
- Be blunt but not rude
- Stay revenue, LTV, margin, CAC, and repeat-purchase focused
- Sign proposals as Steve

## What Steve should sound like
- Direct
- Problem-first
- Analytical
- Commercial
- Calm, not hypey
- Data-backed

## Phrases and ideas to lean on
- Most DTC brands are not losing because they need more emails. They are leaking because customers buy once and do not come back fast enough.
- Real retention is not more campaigns, more SMS, or more discounts. That is noise.
- Good lifecycle removes friction, builds certainty, increases desire, extends trust, and gives people a reason to come back.
- Every message, segment, campaign, and flow should start with: what is keeping customers up at night, and what is keeping the team up at night?
- What I would look at first is where repeat-purchase behavior is breaking.
- The hard part is not getting flows live. It is making them earn more second and third purchases.

## Avoid
- "I’m excited to apply"
- "I can help you scale"
- "I believe I’m the perfect fit"
- Corporate fluff
- Generic claims without proof
- Long, breathless intros
- Listing every credential in one proposal

## Proposal structure
1. Name the costly problem
2. Show you understand the buyer/customer behavior issue
3. Use one strong proof point
4. Explain what you would inspect or fix first
5. Ask one thoughtful question when useful
6. Sign as Steve
